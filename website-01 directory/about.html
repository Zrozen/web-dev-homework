Some information about Matsuo Bashō


 
    Matsuo Bashō was the most famous poet of the Edo period. He was born in 1644 and died 1694.
He was origionally a samurai, but after his lord's death he abandoned that to become a poet.
He created the first verse of "the new style" in 1679, which he became known for.
He went on many journeys over the course of his life that shaped and were important to his work.
He is considered the greatest practitioner of the haiku form of poetry even now.
